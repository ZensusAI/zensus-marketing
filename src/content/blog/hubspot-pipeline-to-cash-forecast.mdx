## What Is a Pipeline Cash Flow Forecast?

<p><strong>A pipeline cash flow forecast estimates when sales opportunities will turn into actual cash deposits.</strong></p>

<p className="mb-2 font-medium text-foreground">Most CRM forecasts answer:</p>

<blockquote className="my-6 border-l-4 border-primary/30 pl-4 not-italic text-muted-foreground">
  <p>How much revenue are we likely to close?</p>
</blockquote>

<p className="mb-2 font-medium text-foreground">A cash flow forecast answers:</p>

<blockquote className="my-6 border-l-4 border-primary/30 pl-4 not-italic text-muted-foreground">
  <p>When will cash arrive in the bank?</p>
</blockquote>

<p>Those are different questions. For founders managing payroll, runway, and fundraising timelines, cash timing is often more important than booked revenue.</p>

## Why Doesn't My Sales Pipeline Equal Cash?

<p><strong>Pipeline measures opportunities. Cash measures money received.</strong></p>

<p>Several events occur between an opportunity appearing in HubSpot and cash reaching your account.</p>

<p className="mb-2 font-medium text-foreground">Typical sequence:</p>

<ol>
  <li>deal enters pipeline</li>
  <li>deal moves to Commit</li>
  <li>contract signed</li>
  <li>contract starts</li>
  <li>invoice issued</li>
  <li>payment terms begin</li>
  <li>customer pays</li>
  <li>cash reaches bank</li>
</ol>

<p>Each step introduces timing delays. This is why large pipelines do not automatically improve cash flow. For why revenue and bank balance diverge on closed deals, see <a href="/blog/arr-vs-cash-for-founders" className="font-medium text-primary underline-offset-4 hover:underline">ARR vs cash for founders</a>.</p>

## How Do You Convert Pipeline to Cash Forecast?

<p><strong>A pipeline cash forecast applies probability and timing assumptions to every deal before estimating expected cash receipts.</strong></p>

<p>The process involves five variables:</p>

| Variable | Why It Matters |
| --- | --- |
| Close probability | Not every deal closes |
| Close date | Determines contract timing |
| Contract start date | Determines billing start |
| Billing structure | Determines invoice amount |
| Payment timing | Determines actual cash arrival |

<p>Many founders only model the first variable. The other four often matter more when you <strong>convert HubSpot deals into expected cash receipts</strong>.</p>

## What Data From HubSpot Should Be Used in a Cash Forecast?

<p>A useful <strong>HubSpot pipeline cash forecast</strong> starts with deal value, stage, close probability, expected close date, contract start date, and billing structure.</p>

### Deal Value and Stage

<p>Example: <strong>$100,000</strong> deal in <strong>Commit</strong> stage.</p>

### Close Probability

<p>Every company should calibrate probabilities using historical conversion rates.</p>

| Stage | Probability |
| --- | ---: |
| Commit | 80% |
| Best Case | 50% |
| Pipeline | 20% |

### Expected Close Date and Contract Start

<p>Example close: <strong>August 15</strong>. Contract start: <strong>September 1</strong>. The start date often matters more than the close date for <strong>CRM to cash forecast</strong> timing.</p>

### Billing Structure

<p className="mb-2 font-medium text-foreground">Examples:</p>

<ul>
  <li>annual prepaid</li>
  <li>quarterly prepaid</li>
  <li>monthly subscription</li>
  <li>Net 30 invoice</li>
</ul>

<p>Billing structure determines when cash hits the bank, not just what HubSpot shows as won revenue.</p>

## How Does Billing Structure Affect Cash Flow Forecasting?

<p><strong>Billing structure determines when revenue becomes cash.</strong></p>

<p>Compare three identical $120,000 contracts.</p>

| --- | ---: |
| Month 1 | $120,000 |
| Month 2 | $0 |
| Month 3 | $0 |

<p>Customer pays $30,000 every quarter.</p>

| Month | Cash Received |
| --- | ---: |
| Month 1 | $30,000 |
| Month 2 | $0 |
| Month 3 | $0 |

### Scenario 3: Monthly Billing

<p>Customer pays $10,000 monthly.</p>

| Month | Cash Received |
| --- | ---: |
| Month 1 | $10,000 |
| Month 2 | $10,000 |
| Month 3 | $10,000 |

<p>Same contract. Completely different cash outcomes. This is <strong>billing terms cash timing</strong> in practice.</p>

## How Should You Weight Pipeline Deals in a Cash Forecast?

<p><strong>Weighted pipeline equals deal value multiplied by close probability.</strong></p>

<p>Formula: <strong>Weighted Value = Deal Value × Probability</strong></p>

| Deal | Value | Probability | Weighted Value |
| --- | ---: | ---: | ---: |
| Deal A | $100,000 | 80% | $80,000 |
| Deal B | $60,000 | 50% | $30,000 |
| Deal C | $40,000 | 25% | $10,000 |

<p>Expected pipeline value: <strong>$120,000</strong></p>

<p>However, <strong>weighted pipeline cash</strong> is still not cash. You must also model timing.</p>
